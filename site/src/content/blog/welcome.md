🏖️ **Kanaha Wind Report** is an automated forecast system for watersport conditions at Kanaha Beach Park on Maui's north shore.

## What you'll find here

Every day, our system pulls data from multiple sources — WeatherFlow sensors, NDBC buoys, NOAA tides, NWS forecasts, and atmospheric models — then an AI analyst interprets the conditions and writes a forecast tailored for:

- 🪁 **Kite foilboarding** — wind, mast, kite, wing, and line recommendations
- 🏄 **Windsurfing** — sail size and session windows
- 🛹 **Wing foiling** — wind and wave assessment
- 🌊 **Downwind foilboarding** — windswell quality and direction analysis

## Equipment recommendations

Each forecast includes specific equipment recommendations based on current conditions — tide-aware mast length, wind-matched kite size, and front/tail wing pairings.

## How it works

Data collection runs every 15 minutes during Hawaiian daylight hours. Forecasts are published twice daily — a morning preview and a midday update with live conditions.

All code is open source. The forecast engine is deterministic — sensors feed scripts, scripts compute analysis, and an LLM writes the narrative.
